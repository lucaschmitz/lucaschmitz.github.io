Ching Shih

The Pirate Queen

Marriage

She was a young woman working in the floating brothel of Canton.

The mighty pirate Zheng Yi noticed her beauty and wished to be with her.

He asked for her hand to make her his most valuable treasure.

Joint Fleet Control

Now husband and wife they led the fleet together.

With her help the fleet grew in power and might.

It rapidly grew from 200 to 600 ships.

Death of the Husband

Sadly, her husband Zheng Yi died after only 6 years of marriage.

Hungry for power she took charge.

She vowed to become a strong pirate lord in honor of her husbands memory.

Laws and Taxes

The mighty pirate queen created laws and taxes for the entire fleet.

Deserters and law breakers where hunted down and punished.

Punishment included cutting off ears, flogginf and quartering.

Naval War

Through the years she earned the name -Terror of South China-

The Chinese, Portuguese and British naval forces all tried their luck.

All of them were unable to defeat the powerful fleet.

Negotiations

Desperate, the Chinese government offered amnesty to pirates.

The only condition was for them to kneel.

Unsatisfied, Ching Shih took matters into her own hands.

Retirement

In the end Ching Shih was victorious once again.

Not only did she get to keep her loot, she got to retire without persecution.

She went back to Canton, opened a gambling house and lived out the rest of her days.
